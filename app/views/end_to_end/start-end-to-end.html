Appeal to an independent tax tribunal

Use this service to challenge decisions by HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC).

The tribunal is independent of government and will listen to both sides of the argument before making a decision.

For more information see the guide to tax tribunals and fees.

You can make an appeal to the tax tribunal using the steps below.

1. Find how much your appeal costs: £20, £50 or £200 (about 5 minutes to complete)

You will need to pay an initial one-off fee to start your appeal with the tribunal. The amount depends on the complexity of your case.

You may also have to pay further fees. You will be told these once the tribunal has looked at your case and decided if it needs a hearing with a judge.

You will get a refund for all tax tribunal fees if the case is decided in your favour.

If you’re on a low income or receiving certain benefits, you may be able to get help with fees.

2. Check if you need a review from HMRC and you’re in time to appeal? (5 minutes)

You must have had a review with HMRC for certain types of appeal.

You must appeal within the time stated on the final decision letter from HMRC. This is usually 30 days.

3. Enter details and save your application if you want to return later (20 minutes)

If you know the fee or want to return and complete an existing appeal – go here.

Save information if you want to return.

You’ll need your decision letter from HMRC.

4. Pay and submit appeal (5 minutes)

Pay the initial fee to process your appeal using a debit or credit card.

If you want to pay by cheque, you have 7 days to pay or your appeal will not be processed. You must pay the fee before the deadline to start an appeal expires.

Other ways to appeal

  • You can submit an appeal or application by paper form
  • Send by post to First-tier Tribunal (Tax Chamber), PO Box 16972, Birmingham, B16 6TZ
