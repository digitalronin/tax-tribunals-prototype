Appeal to an independent tax tribunal

Use this service to challenge decisions by HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC).

The tribunal is independent of government and will listen to both sides of the argument before making a decision.

For more information see the guide to tax tribunals and fees.

You can make an appeal to the tax tribunal by following each step below.

  1. Find out how much your appeal costs (about 5 minutes to complete)

    You will need to pay a fee of £20, £50 or £200 to submit an appeal unless you're eligible for help with fees.

    The initial fee to submit your appeal depends on your type of case.

    You may also have to pay further fees if your case needs needs further documents, correspondence and a hearing in person with a judge.

    The judge has the power to decide whether HMRC should reimburse your fees when they look at your case. The tribunal itself does not have the power to enforce or award costs.

    Check if you're able to get help paying your fees if you’re on a low income or receiving certain benefits.

  2. Check the deadline to appeal to the tribunal (5 minutes)

    You must appeal within the time stated on your last letter from HMRC. This is usually 30 days.

    If you haven't challenged HMRC about your dispute you may wish to resolve the matter directly with HMRC at no extra cost before you appeal to the tribunal.

    For certain types of tax dispute you must first ask HMRC to review your case before you can appeal to the tribunal.

    What you will need

    • date you received your last letter from HMRC
    • reasons why you're outside the time limit if you want to submit an appeal late

  3. Enter your appeal or application details (20 minutes)

    You will need to include grounds for your appeal and upload a scan or photo of the letters from HMRC.

    You'll need to provide:

    • a scan or photo of the notice or decision letter from HMRC
    • HMRC reference number shown on the letters
    • details of your representative if you have one
    • a National Insurance number or VAT registration number depending on your type of appeal

  4. Pay the fee to submit your appeal (5 minutes)

    Your appeal will only be valid once you have paid the initial processing fee

    You can pay:

    • online using a debit or credit card
    • enter a help with fees reference number
    • send a cheque

    What happens next

    The tax tribunal will process your appeal and decide how your case will proceed. You will usually hear within 14 days about what will happen next and whether you need to appear at a hearing with a judge.

Other ways to submit an appeal

  • You can submit an appeal or application by paper form
  • Send your form by post to First-tier Tribunal (Tax Chamber), PO Box 16972, Birmingham, B16 6TZ
