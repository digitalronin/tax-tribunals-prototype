Are in time to appeal to the tax tribunal?

You usually have up to 30 days to appeal a decision from HMRC. When an appeal is completed by HMRC you will receive what's called a 'decision letter'. Use the date from your most recent decision letter to work out whether you are in time to appeal t the tribunal.


Help with types of appeal

HMRC will clearly tell you on the original notice letter sent to you how you can query or appeal against a tax decision.

You normally have 30 days to appeal.

It is free to appeal directly to HMRC. You will usually have to pay your own costs, for example towards postage, printing or professional advice.

If you are disputing a decision about a direct tax, e.g. income tax, PAYE, self assessment, capital gains tax or corporation tax, you will first need to appeal directly to HMRC before you can appeal to an independent tax tribunal.

You can still appeal to the tax tribunal after a direct appeal to HMRC

HMRC will send you a decision letter telling you the outcome of your appeal.

If you disagree with HMRC’s response, you can:

  • ask HMRC for a review of the decision by an officer who wasn’t involved in the original decision (you can still apply to the tax tribunal afterwards if you do not accept the review findings)
  • consider alternative dispute resolution (ADR)
  • appeal to an independent tax tribunal within 30 days

See the guidance from HMRC on how to appeal directly to HMRC about a tax decision.
