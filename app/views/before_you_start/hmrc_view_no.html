Do you want to ask HMRC to review the decision first?

You can challenge the decision with HMRC directly for free.

You must contact HMRC within the deadline shown on the original notice letter from HMRC. This is usually 30 days, although you can still ask for a review if there are good reasons for being late.

There is no fee to pay to challenge the decision directly with HMRC. You will only have to pay your own costs, for example towards postage, printing or legal advice.

You can still appeal to the tax tribunal after a review

HMRC will send you a decision letter telling you the result of the review.

If you disagree with the response from HMRC or cannot find a way to resolve the dispute directly with them, you can appeal to an independent tax tribunal. This is usually 30 days from the date you received the last decision letter from HMRC.

Help with challenging a decision direct with HMRC

HMRC will clearly tell you on the original notice letter sent to you how you can query or appeal against a tax decision.

You normally have 30 days to appeal.

It is free to appeal directly to HMRC. You will usually have to pay your own costs, for example towards postage, printing or professional advice.

You can still appeal to the tax tribunal after an appeal to HMRC

HMRC will send you a decision letter telling you the outcome of your appeal.

If you disagree with HMRC’s response, you can:

  • - ask HMRC for a review of the decision by an officer who wasn’t involved in the original decision (you can still apply to the tax tribunal afterwards if you do not accept the review findings)
  • - consider alternative dispute resolution (ADR)
  • - appeal to an independent tax tribunal within 30 days

See the guidance from HMRC on how to appeal against a tax decision.
